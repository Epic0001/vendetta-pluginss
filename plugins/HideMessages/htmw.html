How to Place a Stock Order

1. Action

Choose whether you want to Buy, Sell, Short or Cover. Select Buy to purchase shares, and Sell when you no longer want to own them. Select Short if you think the stock will go down, and Cover to close your short position.

2. Symbol

Enter the Ticker Symbol to get a quote, company profile and industry. If you don’t know the Symbol of the company, simply start typing the name and a list will populate. Once you select your stock, you will also see a recent price.

3. Quantity

Enter the number of shares you are thinking about buying, then hit TAB and you will see an estimated cost. Feel free to change your number of shares to help you get closer to the amount you want to spend.

4. Order Type

Select an Order Type. A Market order will fill immediately at the current price. Set a Limit order if you only want to pay up to a certain price or sell at a certain price. Use a Stop order below the current price to sell if your stock starts declining in price. Learn more about Order Types.

5. Limit/Stop Price

This specifies the Limit or Stop price that you are using to control when your trade is triggered. It is not available for a Market order.

6. Order Term

For your Limit or Stop orders, you must select the order term which is how long you want the order remain open.

- Good for Day will try to execute until the end of the trading day.

- Good til Cancel will try to execute until you cancel the order.

- Good til Date will try to execute until a specific date (that you must specify).
